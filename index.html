The non-equilibrium characteristics of active matter

Yuting Li (Irene)

Soft matter group, DAMTP

What is active matter?

Locally injecting energy into the system

  1. Cells in tissue, actin filaments
  2. Bacteria suspensions, microtubule bundles
  3. Artificial self propelled particles: Janus particles, shaken rods, walking disks
  4. Fish schools, bird flocks, animal migration

Equilibrium is a very special case

Constraints of equilibrium

  1. Time reversal symmetry
  2. Principle of detailed balance
  3. Boltzmann distribution when connected to a heat bath

Examples of non-equilibrium characteristics

  1. Bacteria in a circular confinement (Ray's group)
  2. Microtubule turbulance -> topological defects in active nematics (experiments by Brandeis group and simulations from Oxford)

Examples of non-equilibrium characteristics

  1. Swarming behaviour in flocking (Simulations of the Toner-Tu model)
  2. long-range order in 2D (violating the equilibrium Wigner-Mermin theorem)

Minimal model for self-propelled bacteria with quorum sensing

Two approches

  1. Explicitly coarse-grain from microscopic rules
  2. Make an intelligent guess

The explicit coarse-graining approach

\begin{align} \partial_t \mathbf{r} & = v(\mathbf{r}) \hat{\mathbf{n}}(\theta)\\ \partial_t \theta & = D_\mathrm{rot} \Lambda \end{align}

The intelligent guess approach

Expand $\rho = \rho_\mathrm{ref} ( 1 + \phi) $

All the terms allowed by symmetry and conservation laws (inc. terms that break detailed balance)

\begin{equation} \begin{split} \partial_t \phi & = - \grad \cdot \mathbf{J} \\ \mathbf{J} & = - M \grad \mu + \zeta (\grad^2 \phi) \grad \phi + \sqrt{2M} \mathbf{\Lambda} \\ \mu & = \alpha \phi + \beta \phi^3 - (\kappa+ \kappa_1 \phi) \grad^2 \phi + \lambda (\grad \phi )^2 \end{split} \end{equation}
