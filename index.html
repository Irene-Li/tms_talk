What is active matter?

  1. Processes that locally injects energy into the environment
  2. Non-equilibrium; no time reversal symmetry; no Boltzmann distribution etc

The intelligent guess approach

Expand $\rho = \rho_\mathrm{ref} ( 1 + \phi) $

Require rotational symmetry, translational symmetry, conservation of $\phi$

\begin{equation} \begin{split} \partial_t \phi & = - \nabla \cdot \mathbf{J} \\ \mathbf{J} & = - M \nabla \mu + \fbox{$\zeta (\nabla^2 \phi) \nabla \phi$} + \sqrt{2M} \mathbf{\Lambda} \\ \mu & = \alpha \phi + \beta \phi^3 - (\kappa+ \kappa_1 \phi) \nabla^2 \phi + \fbox{$\lambda (\nabla \phi )^2$} \end{split} \end{equation}
